Chicken Korma

Description

Chicken Korma is a classic, comforting curry known for its rich, creamy gravy and delicate blend of spices. Tender pieces of chicken are slowly cooked with yogurt, caramelized onions, garlic, ginger, and aromatic spices to create a smooth, flavorful sauce that is mild yet deeply satisfying.
This dish is perfect for both everyday meals and special occasions, offering a balance of warmth and richness that pairs beautifully with naan, roti, or steamed basmati rice. With its fragrant aroma and velvety texture, Chicken Korma is a timeless favorite in South Asian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 3 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil or ghee
  • 2 tablespoons ginger garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 10 to 12 cashews or almonds (optional, soaked and ground)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a pot and fry the sliced onions until they turn golden brown.
  2. Remove half of the fried onions and keep them aside for garnish.
  3. Add the ginger garlic paste to the pot and cook for about one minute.
  4. Add the chicken pieces and cook for several minutes until the chicken changes color.
  5. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, black pepper, and salt and mix well.
  6. Add yogurt and cook on medium heat while stirring so the yogurt blends into the masala.
  7. Add cardamoms, cloves, and cinnamon stick and continue cooking for about 10 minutes.
  8. Add the ground cashew or almond paste if using and mix well to thicken the gravy.
  9. Cover the pot and cook on low heat for about 15 to 20 minutes until the chicken becomes tender and the oil separates from the gravy.
  10. Sprinkle garam masala and the remaining fried onions on top.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with naan or rice. 🍗
