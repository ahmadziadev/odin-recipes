Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken biryani is a rich and aromatic South Asian rice dish made by cooking fragrant basmati rice together with spiced chicken and flavorful herbs. It is known for its layered preparation, where partially cooked rice is placed over a savory chicken masala and then steamed together so the flavors blend perfectly. The dish typically includes ingredients such as yogurt, onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and a mixture of spices like turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, cardamom, cloves, and cinnamon. Fresh herbs such as mint and coriander are often added to enhance the aroma and taste. Chicken biryani is famous for its vibrant color, strong fragrance, and balanced mix of spicy, savory, and slightly tangy flavors. It is one of the most popular and celebrated dishes in Pakistan and India and is commonly served on special occasions, family gatherings, and festive events.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 kg chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 3 onions (thinly sliced)
  • 3 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger garlic paste
  • 3 to 4 green chilies
  • ½ cup cooking oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 to 3 bay leaves
  • 4 green cardamoms
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • Fresh coriander leaves
  • Fresh mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • A pinch saffron soaked in warm milk (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fried onions

Steps

  1. Wash the basmati rice and soak it in water for about 30 minutes.
  2. Boil 6 to 7 cups of water in a large pot and add salt, bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon stick, and cardamom.
  3. Add the soaked rice and cook it until it is about 70% done, then drain the rice and set it aside.
  4. Heat oil or ghee in a large pot and fry the sliced onions until they turn golden brown.
  5. Add ginger garlic paste and cook for about one minute.
  6. Add the chicken pieces and cook until the chicken changes color.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes, yogurt, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, biryani masala, garam masala, and salt.
  8. Cook the mixture for about 10 to 15 minutes until the chicken becomes tender and the oil starts separating from the masala.
  9. Spread half of the cooked rice over the chicken masala.
  10. Add mint leaves, coriander leaves, fried onions, and lemon juice on top of the rice.
  11. Add the remaining rice as the top layer.
  12. Pour the saffron milk over the rice if using.
  13. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on very low heat for about 15 to 20 minutes so the biryani can steam and the flavors combine.
  14. Gently mix the layers before serving and serve hot with raita or salad. 🍛
