Chicken Seekh Kebab

Description

Chicken seekh kebab is a popular South Asian grilled dish made from minced chicken mixed with aromatic spices, herbs, and onions. The mixture is shaped around skewers into long cylindrical kebabs and then grilled, baked, or pan-cooked until juicy and slightly charred. Chicken seekh kebabs are known for their smoky flavor, tender texture, and rich blend of spices. They are commonly served with naan, paratha, or rice along with mint chutney, yogurt sauce, and fresh salad. This dish is widely enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or main meal at restaurants, barbecues, and family gatherings.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams minced chicken
  • 1 small onion (very finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh mint leaves (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • ½ teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons gram flour (besan)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil or butter

Steps

  1. Place the minced chicken in a large bowl.
  2. Add chopped onion, green chilies, coriander leaves, and mint leaves to the chicken.
  3. Add ginger garlic paste, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala, black pepper, lemon juice, gram flour, and salt.
  4. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly until the mixture becomes well combined and slightly firm.
  5. Take a portion of the mixture and shape it around a skewer to form a long cylindrical kebab.
  6. Repeat the process with the remaining mixture.
  7. Heat a grill, oven, or frying pan and lightly grease it with oil.
  8. Cook the kebabs on medium heat while turning them occasionally so they cook evenly on all sides.
  9. Continue cooking for about 10 to 15 minutes until the kebabs are fully cooked and slightly golden or charred.
  10. Remove from heat and serve hot with naan, mint chutney, and salad.
